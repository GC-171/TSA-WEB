Tax Information

Summary

If you install a new energy system that qualifies, and you qualify, you can get up to thirty percent of the cost back in a tax credit.

Qualifications

  • Must be located within the United States
  • Must be as part of an improvement for your home (cannot be for a building solely for business use)
  • Can be one of the following installed:
    • Solar electric panels
    • Solar water heaters
    • Wind turbines
    • Geothermal heat pumps
  • Installed items must be brand new

Price Adjustment

When calculating for the tax credit, the IRS says, “Public utility subsidies for buying or installing clean energy property are subtracted from qualified expenses.”

Rebates are subtracted from qualified expenses if all of these apply as said by the IRS:

  • The rebate is based on the cost of the property.
  • It comes from someone connected to the sale such as the manufacturer, distributor, seller, or installer.
  • It isn't given as payment for services you provide.
